About

A little about me

I'm a biomedical engineering graduate student at the University of Florida, bridging industrial automation and biomedical research. Before grad school, I spent a year at Emerson's Export Engineering Centre designing HMI graphics for pharmaceutical batch systems, leading safety automation subsystems, and conducting DCS validation for oil & gas projects, in environments where precision and compliance weren't optional.

Now at the Neural Interface Technology Lab, I'm developing multi-layer EEG head phantoms to characterize implantable PCB performance in tissue-equivalent environments, alongside pre-clinical animal research. I've also explored the full arc from research to market: medical device commercialization, regulatory strategy, and clinical customer discovery.

My goal is to bring both research depth and industry experience into a medical device career, contributing to products that meaningfully improve patient outcomes. Open to connecting with engineers, clinicians, and teams building the next generation of medical devices.
